Mass-Spring System in 3D

Mass-Spring System in 3D

Configuration

  • $ \mathbf q = \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf x_1 \\ \mathbf x_2 \end{pmatrix} $
  • $ \mathbf {\dot q} = \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf {\dot x}_1 \\ \mathbf {\dot x}_2 \end{pmatrix} $

Kinetic Energy:

$$T = \frac 1 2 \mathbf {\dot q} ^ T \mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q} $$

where $ \mathbf M = \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf M_0 && 0 \\ 0 && \mathbf M_1 \\ \end{pmatrix} $

Potential Energy:

$$V = \frac 1 2 k (l - r)^2 $$

where

$$l = \norm {\begin{pmatrix} -I && I \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf x_1 \\ \mathbf x_2 \end{pmatrix} } = \sqrt {\mathbf {\Delta x}^T \mathbf {\Delta x} } = \sqrt {\mathbf q^T \mathbf B^T \mathbf B \mathbf q } $$

The Lagrangian

$$L = \frac 1 2 \mathbf {\dot q} ^ T \mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q} - \frac 1 2 k (\sqrt {\mathbf q^T \mathbf B^T \mathbf B \mathbf q } - r)^2 $$

Euler-Lagrange Equation

notice that only $ V $ is concerned with $ \mathbf q $, the left side is actually the force.

$$\frac{d}{dt}\frac{\partial L}{\partial \mathbf{\dot{q}}} = - \frac{\partial V}{\partial \mathbf{q}} $$

so, there is

$$\mathbf M \mathbf {\ddot q} = - \frac{\partial V}{\partial \mathbf{q}} $$

Large Mass-Spring System in 3D

Configuration

  • $ \mathbf q = \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf x_1 \\ ... \\ \mathbf x_n \end{pmatrix} $
  • $ \mathbf {\dot q} = \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf {\dot x}_1 \\ ... \\ \mathbf {\dot x}_n \end{pmatrix} $

Kinetic Energy:

$$T = \frac 1 2 \mathbf {\dot q} ^ T \mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q} $$

where $ \mathbf M = \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf M_0 && \cdots && 0 \\ \vdots && \ddots && \vdots \\ 0 && \cdots && \mathbf M_n \end{pmatrix} \in \mathbb R^{3n \times 3n}$

Potential Energy:

$$V = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1}V_j(\mathbf x_A , \mathbf x_B) = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1}V_j(\mathbf E_j \mathbf q) $$

The Lagrangian

$$L = \frac 1 2 \mathbf {\dot q} ^ T \mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q} - \frac 1 2 k (\sqrt {\mathbf q^T \mathbf B^T \mathbf B \mathbf q } - r)^2 $$

Euler-Lagrange Equation

$$\mathbf M \mathbf {\ddot q} = - \frac{\partial V}{\partial \mathbf{q}} \\ - \frac{\partial V}{\partial \mathbf q } = - \frac{\partial}{\partial \mathbf{q}} \sum_{j=0}^{m-1}V_j(\mathbf E_j \mathbf q) = - \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} \frac{\partial}{\partial \mathbf q } V_j(\mathbf E_j \mathbf q) = - \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} \mathbf E_j^T \frac{\partial}{\partial \mathbf q_j} V_j (\mathbf q_j) = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} \mathbf E_j^T \mathbf f_j (\mathbf q_j) $$

therefore

$$\mathbf M \mathbf {\ddot q} = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} \mathbf E_j^T \mathbf f_j (\mathbf q_j) $$

Linearly-Implicit Time Integration

$$\mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q}^{t+1} = \mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q}^t + \Delta t \mathbf f( \mathbf q^{t+1}) \\ \mathbf q^{t+1} = \mathbf q^t + \Delta t \mathbf {\dot q}^{t+1} \\ $$

do approximation

$$\mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q}^{t+1} = \mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q}^t + \Delta t \mathbf f( \mathbf q^{t+1}) = \mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q}^t + \Delta t \mathbf f( \mathbf q^t + \Delta t \mathbf {\dot q}^{t+1}) = \mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q}^t + \Delta t \mathbf f( \mathbf q^t) + \Delta t^2 \frac {\partial {\mathbf f} } {\partial {\mathbf q}} \mathbf {\dot q}^{t+1} \\ \mathbf K = \frac {\partial {\mathbf f} } {\partial {\mathbf q}} = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} [\mathbf E_j^T (-\frac {\partial^2 V_j} {\partial \mathbf q_j^2}) \mathbf E_j] \\ (\mathbf M - \Delta t^2 \mathbf K) \mathbf {\dot q}^{t+1} = \mathbf M \mathbf {\dot q}^t + \Delta t \mathbf f( \mathbf q^t) $$

with Fixed Boundary Conditions
