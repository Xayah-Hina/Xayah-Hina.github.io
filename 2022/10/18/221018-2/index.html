Writing Practice - 04

2022/10/18 English Writing

Today's topic

It is important for people to take risks, both in their professional lives and their personal lives.
Do you think the advantages of taking risks outweigh the disadvantages?
Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.

In modern society, opportunities and challenges abound in our academic, professional, and even daily life. When encountering these, some are willing to take risks to seize opportunities and embrace challenges, while others would prefer a conservative posture. From my point of view, I believe that rational risk-taking is worthwhile after prudent evaluation and analysis, and would bring more benefits than drawbacks during academic careers.

Excellent academic achievement inevitably requires researchers to take many risks. It is well known that a research topic requires considerable innovation and originality, which means expanding and challenging the results of predecessors' research. In this sense, an ambitious researcher who is able to study a bold idea is more likely to produce outstanding work than a stagnant one. To take a famous example, Albert Einstein dared to challenge Newton's classical mechanics and eventually proposed the Theory of Relativity, thereby becoming the most outstanding scientist of the twentieth century.

However, before undertaking a risky behaviour, adequate risk assessment and analysis must get fully considered. In fact, a research topic may take months or even years to study and is extremely prone to failure. Rushing to devote much time to a new, ill-thought-out direction would lead to extremely serious consequences if it failed. For instance, many young Ph.D. students without a deep understanding of their research fields often overestimate their ability to study a new field that is rarely studied, which ultimately leads to delays or even failure to graduate from the Ph.D.

In conclusion, scientific research cannot advance without researchers taking rational risks, but it is also important to be fully prepared to avoid latent dangers as much as possible before taking them.

思考一下展开部分的论述方法……

第一种考虑正反论述 + 多面展开（广度优先）。第一部分写在学业、职业的有利之处，第二部分写学业、职业的不利之处的解决方法。每一部分大概结构会是一句领头句+两句学业两句职业共五句。这么写的问题就是字数大概会到300以上，以及我感觉【结构】的感觉太明显了（领头句+学业的第一句+职业的第二局）感觉内容反而有点不足。

第二种考虑正反论述 + 单面展开（深度优先）。第一部分写在学业的有利之处，第二部分写在学业的不利之处的解决方法。论述部分考虑举例论证、推理论证、对比论证等。这种写法有点考验逻辑掌握功底，如何【紧扣主题】这个要求在紧张的时间限制内还是有点难以驾驭的。另外，单一话题的论述确实容易在论述深度上受人诟病（因为只论述了在学业方面的优劣势）。

第三种考虑一边倒论述 + 多面展开。第一部分写在学业方面的有利之处，第二部分在职业方面的有利之处。论述部分考虑举例论证、推理论证、对比论证等，并在两个一部分尽可能保持【对称】。这样写可塑性比较好，比如托福独立写作要求3段300+字，所以一般是写三段的。采用这种一边倒的论述扩展起来也很方便。但一面倒肯定也有问题，那就是在第一段说了优势>劣势，可全文根本没说劣势……

这篇文章我也考虑了很久不知道怎么下笔。主要是因为题设中有一个【结合自身经历举例】，所以个人还是稍微倾向于第二种论述方法的。（PS. 如果题设没有这句以及在考场环境的话，我大概率会选择第一种，因为写起来快且不容易出错。
